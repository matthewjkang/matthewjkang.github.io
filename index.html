About Me

I was born in San Ramon, California, the third son of two Korean immigrants.

Growing up, I was very active and curious. On weekends, my parents would take me to the library or to church. I loved to read, hike, and play pokemon. (I always had my gameboy with me wherever I went.) I was a boy scout, a varsity wrestler, and played in the school's marching band.

Currently, I live in Berkeley. Prior, I lived in Pleasant Hill.

I have lived in the East Bay for my entire life, excluding the time I spent in San Diego for college.

On the weekends, I love to go on roadtrips, explore new restaurants, and attend local events like fairs and farmers markets.

For transplants or those visiting SF, I recommend checking out the Marin Headlands, the Palace of Fine Arts, the Sutro Baths, Twin Peaks, the Japanese Tea Garden, the MOMA, the Legion of Honor, and the Alameda Antiques Fair.

I have a spreadsheet of restaurants that I have rated around the Bay. You can find it here.

I can speak English, French, and Korean. I am a dual citizen of the United States and South Korea.
